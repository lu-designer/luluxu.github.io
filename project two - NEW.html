Challenge

SJET Student Journey Experience Taskforce is a program initiated by Macquarie University in the year of 2017. The objective of the program is to look at student experiences as a whole. Through foundational user research, it was discovered that Marquarie students experience a lack of belonging to the university, particularly after the online/hybrid learning model was introduced due to the pandemic. Students longe for meaningful interactions with their peers & academic staff to feel a stronger sense of belonging.

EMPATHISE

Quantitative research

Prior to conducting further user research, initial product concepts were explored based on the user insights generated from the foundational research.

In order to validate the research insights & product concepts and further explore product opportunities, we conducted quantatitive research to current Macquarie students and students from other Australian universities.

Survey specifics

Two separate surveys were created for MQ students & other uni students to uncover any gaps.

In survey, participants were asked to give their definitions of belonging, their experiences of working with others for academic projects & hobby projects, their experiences of university campus, university social programs such as mentor/mentee program, inclusive programs and more. Lastly, participants were asked to rate their likelihood to use a mobile application with features that aim to help them gain a sense of belonging at university.

INSIGHT SYNTHESIS

Research insight

Through synthesising survey results from both Macquarie uni participants & other uni participants, following insights began to emerge:

  • Participants considered a sense of community , feeling welcomed, feeling safe to express themselves and building friendship of great importance to feel belonged.
  • Participants longed for the opportunities to meet more peers with similar degrees of studies and common interests.
  • When it comes to university communications, it is essential to create a single source of truth rather than scattered information.
  • For social event communication, students expect to be notified with information of relevance (e.g., related to their studies/interests) rather than having to search for it.
  • There are substantial needs for students to collaborate with their peers on hobby & passion projects as a way to meet like-minded peers & build friendship outside of classroom. This in return helps them to develop a sense of community.

IDEATION & PROTOTYPES

Product concept

Overview

Based on user insights, we identified a product opportunity of a mobile application that meets user needs of feeling belonged & experiencing a sense of community through personalised university communications, online & offline socialisation with peers of same studies and common interests to build friendships through shared interests and meaningful interactions.

Key features
  • Personalisation at the start: survey at the start of app to collect personal information, such as degree of study, interests, special needs and more. Students receive ongoing communication based on their needs & interests.
  • Personalised landing page: students easily access contents that are relevant to their interests / needs on their personalised landing page.
  • Cultivating a sense of belonging for online students: this feature allows online learners to socialise with other Macquarie students in their local areas through study or hobby activities.
  • Passion project collaboration: students can interact with peers in a meaningful way by collaborating on a variety of projects, such as design hackathon, short film, guitar jam to name just a few.
  • Connecting to Macquarie campus: while getting to know all the featured buildings & facilities at Macquarie, students can easily initiate an on-campus event, such as a study group, a sport game or a coffee catch-up to mingle with their peers and get to know Macquarie campus.
