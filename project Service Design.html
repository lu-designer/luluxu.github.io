Challenge

SJET Student Journey Experience Taskforce is a program initiated by Macquarie University in the year of 2017. The objective of the program is to look at student experiences as a whole. The Starting Episode is one of the major component of SJET program and it focuses on accepting offer & enrolling experiences to identify student’s pain points and uncover opportunities to improve student's university experiences.

Discovery

Through three workshops with internal stakeholders, we mapped out the blueprint of student journey & key service touch points.

Co-design workshop

In co-design workshop, internal stakeholders were invited to participate in the service design process to map out the key steps and important factors in the accepting offer & enrolling stage of student journey.

↑ CO-DESIGN WORKSHOP - MIRO BOARD

Competitor workshop

In competitor workshop, stakeholders used green and red dots to vote for the service (digital & analogue) components from other universities they like & dislike

↑ COMPETITOR WORKSHOP - DOT-VOTING ON MIRO

Mapping the student journey

Student experience journey map captures the typical streamlined journey from accepting offer to enrolling into courses that the domestic & international students at Macquarie University are expected to experience. It also maps out the service touch points, such as comms communication, current student websites, student systems & student service centre at each step and how to integrate these service touch points effectively in a long run.

↑ JOURNEY MAP

Development

Through examining current student website, the foundational user research report done previously and communications through other service channels, e.g., comms EDMs & service connect, following gaps have been identified:

  • Step-by-step guides for enrolling & getting students started are missing from current student portal on Macquarie website
  • Multiple webpages about enrolling have duplicated information and are confusing to navigate
  • Information users receive from other channels, e.g., student emails do not align with what's on Macquarie website

↑ COMPETITOR WORKSHOP

↑ CO-DESIGN WORKSHOP

Outcome

The exploration of the student journey blueprint, which mapped out key steps and service touchpoints, along with a gap analysis between the current state ("as-is") and the desired future state ("to-be"), propelled the project team into the next phase of development.
